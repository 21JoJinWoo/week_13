Bookshelves were perfectly arranged, and the air lay still.

Only the sunlight spilling through the window softly lit the room, as if time itself had paused. Ink smudges glittered on a single sheet wedged between the pages, and on the dust-laden desk lay a well-used pen, abandoned mid-thought.

Someone was preparing for an exam, and someone was dreaming.

On one side of the desk, a stack of textbooks bore densely penciled formulas and key points, while a small radio beside them murmured a quiet classical melody. On the cushions across the bed, travel snapshots and handwritten letters lay scattered. Some plotted their success, some yearned for freedom—each sketching tomorrow in the calm of an ordinary day.

In that order, which seemed too perfect ever to vanish, no one sensed the tide of unease.

The ticking of the wall clock seemed poised to break the silence, yet the sound never returned. The school bell down the hallway, the hum of engines on the street—every sound held its breath.

A single book trembled. Just once.

The bookshelf door creaked closed, sending a few loose pages fluttering to the floor. Only then did ripples stir in the long-dormant space.

Seaside – A Child's Sandcastle
That day, the sea was peaceful.
Sunlight shimmered over the waves, and the tide breathed in quiet rhythm. The salty air was warm, and a gentle mist blurred the horizon. The world looked as if nothing could ever go wrong.
The child built a castle on the shore,
and the sand didn't seem like it could ever break.
With tiny, diligent hands, the child shaped towers and walls, wet sand packed firm beneath fingertips. The waves crept close now and then, but the child didn't mind. To them, the castle was a promise — a whole world held in place by imagination and play.
The sky was blue, and the wind
was full of mischief.
Clouds drifted like cotton, and the breeze tousled the child's hair. Seagulls cried above, and the waves hummed like a lullaby. It was a moment untouched by noise, fear, or shadow — perfectly whole.
But beyond the horizon,
a massive wave was rising.
No one saw it coming. Far away, the wind had shifted, and something deep within the sea had begun to stir. The wave grew, silently, steadily, moving ever closer beneath the illusion of calm.
The sandcastle said nothing,
even at the end.
It held its place, even as it knew it would fall. No cries, no resistance — only quiet collapse beneath the breath of the sea.

APARTMENT CALM OUTSIDE THE WINDOW

IT WAS AN ORDINARY EVENING. THE SKY OUTSIDE THE WINDOW WAS CLEAR AND DISTANT, SPOTLESS IN ITS SERENITY—YET NO ONE NOTICED. ON THE MUTED TELEVISION, THE NEWS PLAYED SILENTLY. THE CHILD SAT HUNCHED OVER, COMPLETELY IMMERSED IN THEIR GAME. IN THE KITCHEN, THE SCENT OF FRIED RICE LINGERED IN THE AIR, AND THE SOUND OF DISHWATER FORMED A STEADY RHYTHM. EVERYTHING FELT NORMAL, AND THAT SENSE OF NORMALCY WAS UNSHAKABLE.

EVERYONE'S GAZE REMAINED LOW, ANCHORED IN THEIR ROUTINES. NOT A SINGLE PERSON THOUGHT TO LIFT THEIR HEAD AND LOOK UP AT THE SKY. THE SKY, HOWEVER, QUIETLY BORE A SIGN—AN OMEN—UNFOLDING BEYOND THEIR AWARENESS. AND WITHOUT WARNING, IT DREW A LINE THROUGH THE QUIET EVENING.

A SINGLE BEAM OF LIGHT TORE THROUGH THE SKY AND CAME CRASHING DOWN. ONLY THEN DID WE UNDERSTAND: THE SKY, WHICH WE HAD FAILED TO LOOK AT, HAD CHANGED FIRST.
